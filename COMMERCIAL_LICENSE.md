# Cyber Fidget — Commercial Licensing

## Open Source Use

The Cyber Fidget firmware is licensed under GPL-3.0. This means you **can** use it commercially, but your modifications to the firmware must also be released under GPL-3.0 with source code available.

Apps built through the HAL API are exempt from this requirement (see [PERMISSIONS.md](PERMISSIONS.md)).

## When You Might Need a Commercial License

- **OEM integration**: Embedding Cyber Fidget firmware in a commercial product without open-sourcing your modifications.
- **Proprietary firmware forks**: Creating a closed-source derivative of the firmware (not just an app).
- **White-label products**: Selling devices based on Cyber Fidget hardware under your own brand.

## Hardware Design Files

PCB schematics, layout files, and BOMs are not published under an open-source license during active development. Commercial use of the hardware design requires a separate agreement.

See [ABANDONMENT.md](ABANDONMENT.md) for conditions under which hardware designs are released under CERN-OHL-S-2.0.
